# revised vs refinied
## Definition
- Revise :Revised means changed, corrected, or updated.
- Refine :Refined means improved in quality, precision, or elegance.

## Recipe Analogy

- **Revised**  
  - Meaning: You **change the recipe itself**.  
  - Action: Swap ingredients, adjust cooking time, rewrite steps.  
  - Example: "The cake was too dry, so I revised the recipe by adding more butter and reducing baking time."  

- **Refined**  
  - Meaning: You **perfect the execution of the recipe**.  
  - Action: Make small improvements for better taste, texture, or presentation.  
  - Example: "I refined the cake by whisking the batter more carefully and garnishing it nicely."  

**Memory Tip:**  
- **Revise = Change the recipe**  
- **Refine = Perfect the cooking**

# Construction vs Implmentation vs apply
## Definition
- Construction:Building, assembling, or creating something based on a solution, plan, or design.
- Implmentation:Executing or applying the solution so that it actually works.
- Apply: Apply means to use or put into practice something in a real situation.It is the act of taking a concept, method, plan, or solution and making it work in a specific context.

## Explanation
- Apply ⊆Implementation ⊆ Construction

## Analogy
- **Apply**  
  - Meaning: **just use**  
  - Example: Use the algorithm to process data  

- **Implement**  
  - Meaning: **use + make it work**  
  - Example: Write code and run the algorithm on the data  

- **Construct**  
  - Meaning: **use + make it work + make it usable**  
  - Example: Build the full system from scratch, including setup, code, tests, and user interface


# Tuning vs configuration
## Definition
- Tuning:Tuning is the process of optimizing or adjusting a system’s parameters to improve performance, efficiency, or responsiveness.Usually involves small, iterative changes while the system is running or tested.
- Configuration:Configuration is the process of setting up a system, software, or device according to specific requirements or preferences.

It usually involves choosing options, parameters, or settings before or during operation.


# Configuration vs Tuning (Coffee Analogy)

- **Configuration**  
  - Meaning: **Setting up the system correctly before use**  
  - Analogy: Preparing your coffee before drinking — adding coffee powder, sugar, milk, water.  
  - Example: You decide the recipe for your coffee: 2 spoons sugar, 1 spoon coffee powder, 200ml milk.  

- **Tuning**  
  - Meaning: **Adjusting, optimizing, or improving while in use**  
  - Analogy: While drinking, you notice it’s too sweet or too weak — so you adjust by adding/removing sugar or coffee powder.  
  - Example: Add a little sugar, remove excess milk, or stir more to improve taste.  

✅ Key Points:  
- **Configuration = initial setup / baseline**  
- **Tuning = iterative adjustments to improve performance or taste**

# Compile vs Combine
## Definition
- Compile(non-programming):To produce something (like a list, book, or report) by collecting and organizing information from multiple sources.

Focuses on gathering, processing, and arranging material into a structured whole.
- Compile(programming):To translate or transform something into another form, often from human-readable or high-level form into machine-readable or lower-level form.

Focuses on processing each individual part according to rules to produce a result.

- Combine:To join two or more things together without necessarily reorganizing or processing them.

# Compile vs Combine (Analogy)

- **Compile**  
  - Meaning: Collect, organize, and produce a structured output from multiple sources.  
  - Analogy: You want to make the **ultimate coffee guide**. You **collect recipes from different friends, books, and blogs**, organize them by type, and create a **single well-structured guide**.  
  - Key: Gathering + organizing + structuring  

- **Combine**  
  - Meaning: Simply join or merge things together.  
  - Analogy: You want to **make a cup of coffee**. You **combine coffee powder, sugar, milk, and water** — nothing is curated or reorganized, just mixed.  
  - Key: Joining / merging only
